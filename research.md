## Research

My research develops and applies **simulation-based inference (SBI)** methods for **experimental evolution**. I combine deep learning, stochastic simulations, and Bayesian statistics to extract biological insights from complex experimental data.

### The Collective Posterior

<div class="card">
  <h3>Inferring Parameters from Highly Variable Experimental Replicates</h3>
  <p>
    High-throughput experimental evolution generates many independent replicates, each producing
    noisy and variable outcomes. I developed the <strong>collective posterior</strong> — a method
    that aggregates information across replicates using a robust product-of-experts scheme.
    This approach automatically downweights outlier replicates, achieving superior accuracy
    and efficiency compared to existing methods.
  </p>

### Collaborations with Experimental Evolution Labs

<div class="card">
  <h3>Copy Number Variation in Yeast Evolution</h3>
  <p>
    In collaboration with the Gresham Lab (NYU), I apply SBI to study the evolutionary dynamics
    of copy number variants (CNVs) in yeast. Our work has revealed that DNA template switching
    during replication (ODIRA) is the predominant mechanism generating adaptive gene amplifications,
    and that segmental duplications remain remarkably stable over hundreds of generations
    even without selection pressure.
  </p>

<div class="card">
  <h3>RNA Virus Evolution</h3>
  <p>
    Using neural posterior estimation, we inferred mutation rates, selection coefficients, and
    epistatic interactions from RNA virus haplotype data. This work demonstrated that SBI
    can extract rich evolutionary parameters from viral sequencing data that are inaccessible
    to traditional statistical methods.
  </p>
